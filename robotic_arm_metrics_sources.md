# Best Sources for Robotic Arm Manipulation Performance Metrics

Based on recent web searches, here is a curated list of top sources providing performance metrics for robotic arm manipulation. These include standards, benchmarks, and research papers focused on metrics like accuracy, speed, force control, and more.

1. **NIST Special Publication 1227: Performance Metrics and Test Methods**  
   - Source: [NIST](https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/SpecialPublications/NIST.SP.1227-draft.pdf)  
   - Published: Draft  
   - Focus: Metrics for grasping, manipulation, and assembly in robotics, including test methods for robot hands.

2. **Measuring Performance: Metrics for Manipulator Design, Control, and Optimization**  
   - Source: [MDPI](https://www.mdpi.com/2218-6581/12/1/4)  
   - Published: 2022  
   - Focus: Comprehensive survey of mechanical performance metrics for industrial manipulators.

3. **Benchmarking Protocols for Evaluating Small Parts Robotic Assembly Systems**  
   - Source: [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7537423/)  
   - Published: 2020  
   - Focus: Protocols and metrics for assessing robotic assembly performance, including force-based operations.

4. **Optimal Design of High-Dynamic Robotic Arm Based on Angular Momentum Maximum**  
   - Source: [IEEE Xplore](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10884838)  
   - Published: Recent  
   - Focus: Introduces angular momentum as a key metric for high-dynamic robotic arms.
